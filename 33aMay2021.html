An Update on Recompiling Your C++ Plugins for InDesign

Last month we said that InDesign developers would need to recompile C++ plugins to keep them working on InDesign v16.3+ on macOS. We’ve updated our blog article; although a recompile is recommended for macOS computers running on Intel processors, it is not mandatory.

We highly recommend testing your InDesign plugins in the 16.3 prerelease of InDesign. The SDK is available now.

If you want your C++ InDesign plugins to run natively on InDesign for Apple silicon, you will need to compile them using XCode 12.3 or higher, otherwise, users can access your plugin using Rosetta 2 mode.

COVID-19 Impact in India

As you know, the second wave of COVID-19 in India has led to a major health crisis affecting the lives of millions of people, including many of our Adobe employees and their families. We’re trying to provide the support and flexibility our colleagues in India need during this challenging time. Working with other US-based organizations, Adobe is helping to provide concrete aid, including donations to organizations like the American India Foundation and the Akshaya Patra Foundation.

During this difficult time, we ask for your patience. We have told our employees in India to prioritize their health and that of their families. This may result in delays for product updates, new APIs and developer resource updates, and longer review cycles for plugins and integrations. To make sure our colleagues in India have the chance to attend, we have also postponed Creative Cloud Partner Days from the end of June to the second half of July (you will hear more about this in the next newsletter). We hope this situation will have as little impact on you as possible, but we appreciate your understanding and partnership.
